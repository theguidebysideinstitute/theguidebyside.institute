Why It's Different

Three habits we're retiring from Indian teacher training.

Old: the seminar lecture

Learning by doing

Micro-practice cycles replace passive, one-off seminars — teachers build the artifact in the room, not just watch a slide about it.

Old: theory that vanishes by Monday

Built for Indian classrooms

Contextualised for syllabus pressure, large batches and mixed abilities — not the well-resourced classrooms most training assumes.

Old: teachers as a checklist

A whole-community rollout

Every engagement aligns leaders, teachers and parents together — because adoption fails when any one group is left out.

Old: the tool teaches the child

Human cognition, protected

Students stay the author of their own thinking. AI is taught as a sparring partner for ideas, never a substitute for them.

Curriculum Architecture

Same micro-modules. Three different formats.

Every GuidebySide module is built once, at 60–90 minutes, then re-assembled to fit the room in front of us — a single teacher's weekend, a school's six-month engagement, or an hour with parents on a Tuesday evening. Choose a format to see how the modules snap together.

Designed against a compliance backbone, not an afterthought.

Every module we write maps explicitly to a CBSE guideline or NEP 2020 goal — from Competency-Based Assessment to Acceptable Use Policy. It's the difference between a workshop and something your HR can log as a recognised appraisal input.
